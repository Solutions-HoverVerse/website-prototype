Healthcare · Hover Health™

Touchless clinical workstations that break a pathogen vector.

Shared keyboards, mice and touchscreens on Workstations on Wheels (WOWs) are touched dozens of times per shift and travel room to room — a documented mobile fomite shown to harbor MRSA, VRE and C. difficile. Hover Health replaces them with a voice-plus-gesture interface on Hover OS: remove the touched surface, remove a cross-contamination vector that drives healthcare-associated infections.

WOW

Starts with the highest-risk surface

The Workstation on Wheels ships first — the prime cross-contamination vector in acute units, with badge tap-and-go SSO and constant room-to-room travel.

EHR

Burn recovery & physical therapy

Rehabilitation that never has to touch healing skin.

Recovery depends on precise, longitudinal tracking of range of motion and limb extension — but goniometers and strap-on wearables add friction and risk tearing grafts on hyper-sensitive skin. HoverVerse turns the therapy room into a contactless, data-rich diagnostic space: deterministic depth-vision tracks skeletal movement with no markers, no wearables and no contact, while gamified targets keep patients engaged.

100% contactless tracking

Sub-centimetre skeletal and joint-angle telemetry with zero patient-worn markers or skin tethers — nothing touches healing tissue or grafts.

Upper & lower limb, two axes

A horizontal wall surface gamifies upper-limb reaching; an overhead downward camera performs contactless goniometry on strides, leg extensions and gait clearance.

Automated, longitudinal data

Every session logs precise coordinates; SurfaceWare Central aggregates anonymized progress and maps it into EHR analytics (e.g. Cerner PowerChart) over standard web APIs.

Biosafety labs & cleanrooms

In a sterile zone, a single touch breaks the barrier.

In BSL-3/BSL-4 labs and ISO Class 1–9 cleanrooms, keyboards, mice and touchscreens break air barriers, strain heavily-suited personnel, and act as persistent vectors for cross-contamination and particulate ingress. HoverVerse replaces them with touchless, multimodal control — gestures tracked through the containment sash, the UI short-throw-projected onto the workbench or rear wall. Fully offline at the edge, LIMS-integrated and validation-ready.

Protects the laminar air curtain

No reaching out of the safety cabinet for a mouse. Hands are tracked through the glass sash at 91–98% command accuracy, so the air barrier is never broken.

Ends the disposable barrier economy

No plastic keyboard sleeves or mouse wraps, no doffing gloves to type at an external terminal — reclaiming the 15–25% hands-on-bench efficiency that gowning friction destroys.

Validation-ready & fully offline

Sensors in 316L stainless / PTFE housings, UI projected onto sterile surfaces, computing offline at the edge with encrypted LAN to LIMS — built for FDA 21 CFR Part 11 and ISO/IEC 17025.
